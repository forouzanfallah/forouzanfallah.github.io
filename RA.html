Graduate Research Assistant
Tehran, Iran
Sep. 2018 - Jul. 2022

My fascination with machine learning technologies was not just serendipitous; it stemmed from witnessing the sheer transformative potential these tools offer. Diving deep into the intricate web of algorithms and neural architectures, I dedicated countless hours understanding the intricacies of reinforcement learning, supervised learning models like Support Vector Machines and Random Forests, and unsupervised models like clustering algorithms. This expansive study allowed me to develop predictive models that could adapt and evolve with data, ensuring high levels of accuracy and efficiency. Alongside this, the integration of advanced optimization techniques further augmented the capabilities of my models, ensuring robustness even in uncertain environments.

During my master's journey, I distinctly branched my focus into two areas:

Simulation: Here, my work with Matlab-Simulink transcended basic system design. I implemented state-space models, controller designs, and system identification procedures to develop dynamic simulations. By leveraging feedback control loops, I ensured systems exhibited desired transient and steady-state behaviors. Further, by integrating these simulations with real-world data, I was able to validate and refine models iteratively, ensuring their viability in real-world scenarios.

Applied Machine Learning: Beyond theoretical constructs, I took a keen interest in practical applications. My projects spanned from developing deep learning models for image recognition to using recurrent neural networks for time series analysis. Additionally, I delved into fault detection using anomaly detection algorithms and achieved fault-tolerant control by employing hybrid models that could self-repair by learning from previous faults. This holistic approach meant that not only were the systems I worked on efficient, but they were also resilient and adaptable.
